Sept 7 (Sun) · Day 1

Shiba · Tsukiji · Ginza

Morning

Kyu-Shiba-rikyu Garden (旧芝離宮恩賜庭園)

Closest point to hotel. 20-30 min, up to 45 min if sitting by the pond. Small Edo-period garden, central pond with islands, rest pavilion (azumaya).

Map
Morning

Hamarikyu Gardens (浜離宮恩賜庭園)

45 min, up to 1.5-2 hrs. Entry fee 300 yen. Contains Nakajima-no-Ochaya teahouse floating on the tidal pond (matcha + wagashi), a 300-year-old pine tree held up by supports, and a plum grove.

Map
Midday

Tsukiji Outer Market (築地場外市場)

45 min, up to 1.5 hrs. ~400 food stalls plus Tsukiji Masamoto knife shop inside. Tamagoyaki skewer, amazake, fresh uni, grilled scallop.

Sept 8 (Mon) · Day 2

Zojoji · Nihonbashi · Hibiya

Morning

Zojoji Temple (増上寺) + Shiba Park (芝公園)

1.5-2 hrs. 400-year-old Sangedatsumon gate (national cultural asset), field of Jizo statues in knit hats behind the main hall, shogun burial grounds within the wider Shiba Park grounds.

Map
Midday

Hibiya Park (日比谷公園) + Imperial Palace East Gardens (皇居東御苑)

2.5-3 hrs. Hibiya Park alone is quick; East Gardens next door add the Nijubashi double bridge, Sakuradamon Gate, and old castle moats.

Map

ALT

Tsujihan (Nihonbashi original)

Per Gal (ptitim.com): insanely good kaisendon (seafood rice bowl), worth the wait - though it can run close to 2 hours. Less crowded branches exist in Kagurazaka and Akasaka if you'd rather skip the line.
